Sumatran Elephants

Sumatran Elephants

Sumatran elephants feed on a variety of plants and deposit seeds wherever they go, contributing to a healthy forest ecosystem. They also share their lush forest habitat with several other endangered species, such as the Sumatran rhino, tiger, and orangutan, and countless other species that all benefit from an elephant population that thrives in a healthy habitat.

Fact about Sumatran Elephants

Type: Mammal.
Diet: Herbivore.
Life span: 55-70 years.
Weight: 3,000-5,000 kg (6,500-11,000lbs)
Size: Height 1.9m -2.7m (5ft-9ft)
Habitat: Broadleaf moist tropical forests.
Range: Sumatra and Borneo, Indonesia.
Scientific name: Elephas maximus sumatrensis

What is special about the Sumatran elephant ?

Sumatran elephants reach a shoulder height of between 2 and 3.2 m (6.6 and 10.5 ft), weigh between 2,000 and 4,000 kg (4,400 and 8,800 lb), and have 20 pairs of ribs. Their skin colour is lighter than of maximus and indicus with the least depigmentation
